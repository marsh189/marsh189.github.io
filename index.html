About Me

I studied Computer Science with a minor in Game Development Programming at Chapman University. I received my degree in May of 2018. I worked for Chapman University's IS&T department as a Web Development Assistant. As a Web Development Assistant, I had the task of improving and maintaining the school's web and mobile applications using ASP.NET and C#.

Throughout my game development studies, I enjoyed working on the User Interface and triggered effects. As a gamer, I have seen games that do not have an interface that is up to par with the game, so while I make my own games, I make sure the menus look just as nice and work just as well as the rest of the game. I also love making the player trigger certain events. This includes the player killing an enemy with a gun, picking up an item, or falling to their deaths. Most of my game developing experience has been with Unity, although I have some experience using Unreal and their blueprint system.

I currently work as a Lead Istructor for MVCode in the San Francisco Bay Area. As an instructor, I help kids learn the fundamentals of Computer Science. We start off by teaching them how to build games and projects in JavaScript Processing (p5.js). They then can move on to some more advanced aspects of CS, such as Web Design (HTML/CSS) or Game Development (Unity).
