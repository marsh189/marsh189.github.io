Gift Giving Application

This application was a prototype for a project in a Human Computer Interaction class. The concept we were trying to reach was an app that helps you purchase gifts for friends for certain occasions. Your profile has an item wishlist that you can update with items that you would like your friends to get you. Then, your friends can mark that they purchased an item for you from your wishlist. To make this application unique, we wanted to have the items purchased for you hidden from you so that you do not know what item you will receive, but others can see it and not buy you a duplicate item.

After user testing by classmate, it was determined that the best course for the application would to have it be used for certain occasions, such as Christmas or a wedding. This brought us to make a Secret Santa mode. When you create a new Secret Santa group, the application will choose a random person for each person in the group for them to purchase a gift for. This way, nobody will have a clue who is purchasing a gift for whom.

What Was Implemented

During our one week of production, I took the lead on building as much of the app as possible. Using Android Studio, I constructed the layout of most of the main pages needed. After I had the look setup correctly, I implemented the bottom navigation bar and setup a local database for login testing.

What Would Come Next

If we had more time to work on the application, the next step would have been to have the user be able to fill their lists (Friends, Wishlist, Groups). We were working on this at the end of the class but ran out of time. After that was inplemented, we would then find a way to host a database so everyone would have access to the same data.
